# Nexus Letter Brief — Vietnam Combat Engineer, 1965–1969

**Patient:** Co. A, **70th Engineer Battalion (Combat)**, "Kodiaks" / 937th Engr Group / 18th Engr Bde · MOS 12B Pioneer · Aug 1965 – Oct 1969 RVN · II Corps (An Khe → Pleiku → Ban Me Thuot) · APO SF 96450 · Vietnam Service Medal · honorable. **No significant in-service medical issues.**

**Conditions:** hypertension · ischemic heart disease · stroke · seizure · renal mass (suspect RCC) · urinary incontinence.

---

## Exposure profile (unit-documented)

- **Tactical herbicides (TCDD)** — boots-on-ground RVN ⇒ presumed exposure under **38 C.F.R. § 3.307(a)(6)(iii)**. Unit built/maintained base perimeters where ground-based herbicide spraying was standard ([Stellman, *Sci Total Environ* 2015](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25531592/)).
- **TCE / chlorinated solvents** — daily motor-pool degreasing. **IARC Group 1 — sufficient evidence for kidney cancer** ([IARC Mono 106](https://publications.iarc.who.int/_publications/media/download/3808/35dbe800bc961a10150e81ba239717d03dae477e.pdf)).
- **JP-4 / benzene** — POL operations at Camp Radcliff (13,600+ drums), Pleiku AB, Camp Holloway. RCC case-referent **OR 3.1–3.5**.
- **Burn-pit / open-air waste burning** — documented at Vietnam base camps ([GAO-25-107504, May 2025](https://www.gao.gov/assets/gao-25-107504.pdf)). Note: PACT Act burn-pit *presumption* does **not** cover Vietnam — use as direct-exposure evidence only.
- **Crystalline silica** (quarry ops), **asbestos** (heavy-equipment friction products + Transite), **lead** (MOGAS, paints, ordnance), **PCBs** (transformer installs), **blast/concussive TBI** (mortar attacks at Camp Radcliff; convoy ambushes — DSC awarded to a 70th NCO 13 Mar 1968).

## Two presumptive anchors — already on the § 3.309(e) list

| Condition | Basis | Mechanism (citable) |
|---|---|---|
| **Hypertension** | **PACT Act § 404 (2022)**; NASEM AO Update 2018: *"sufficient evidence of association"* | TCDD/AhR → eNOS inactivation, AT1R upregulation, RAAS sensitization, oxidative endothelial injury ([Kopf, PMC](https://ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/sites/ppmc/articles/PMC2648846/)) |
| **Ischemic heart disease** | Presumptive since 2010 (75 FR 53202) | AhR-driven accelerated atherosclerosis; Seveso male cardiovascular RR 2.00 ([NASEM CV chapter](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK195089/)) |

> § 3.309(e) Note: stroke is **excluded** from "ischemic heart disease" — pursue stroke as **secondary**, not as part of IHD.

---

## Downstream conditions — secondary connection (38 C.F.R. § 3.310)

**Stroke ← HTN + IHD.** HTN: 2–6× ischemic / 2–7× hemorrhagic stroke risk ([HTN-stroke meta, PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12823101/)). Backup direct theory: NASEM Update 2014 — *"limited or suggestive"* for direct herbicide-stroke link; Illinois vets cohort ischemic stroke OR 1.34 (1.28–1.41) ([AHA Stroke 2024](https://www.ahajournals.org/doi/10.1161/str.55.suppl_1.WP249)).

**Seizure ← Stroke (PSE).** Post-stroke epilepsy = **30–50% of new-onset seizures in adults ≥ 60** ([Neurology 2024](https://www.neurology.org/doi/10.1212/WNL.0000000000209450) · [PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9629926/)). Alternative: combat TBI (PTE) — multi-decade latency ([Vietnam Head Injury Study, PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3093742/)).

**Incontinence ← Stroke.** Post-stroke neurogenic bladder: **38–60% acute, 15–25% chronic; ~90% detrusor overactivity** ([PMC 2024](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11409650/)).

**Renal mass / RCC — direct (Combee) + secondary to HTN.** Not on AO presumptive list; *Combee v. Brown*, 34 F.3d 1039 (Fed. Cir. 1994) preserves direct theory. Five converging carcinogenic pathways:

1. **TCE — IARC Group 1**, sufficient evidence for human kidney cancer; S-DCVC nephrotoxic metabolite is mutagenic ([NTP RoC](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK575906/)).
2. **JP-4 / benzene — RCC OR 3.1–3.5** ([VA Public Health](https://www.publichealth.va.gov/exposures/petroleum/jet_fuels.asp)).
3. **TCDD** — pooled meta-analysis (31 cohorts, n=29,605) significant kidney-cancer mortality elevation ([Xu, *Sci Rep* 2016](https://www.nature.com/articles/srep38012)).
4. **HTN — RCC OR ~2.0** baseline, **OR 2.6–4.1 after 25 yr** ([Chow, NEJM 2000](https://www.nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJM200011023431804); [Colt, PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8109699/)). Since HTN is service-connected, RCC is secondary-connectable through HTN.
5. **Silica** — quarry exposure → silica nephropathy ([PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3266824/)).

RCC latency 20–50 yr — squarely consistent with 1960s exposure / current presentation.

## One-line legal framing per condition

| Dx | Frame the opinion as… |
|---|---|
| HTN | Presumptive (38 C.F.R. § 3.309(e), per PACT Act) — opinion optional but useful for downstream chain |
| IHD | Presumptive (38 C.F.R. § 3.309(e)) |
| Stroke | Secondary to HTN + IHD (§ 3.310(a) and (b)); backup direct (NASEM "limited/suggestive") |
| Seizure | Secondary to stroke (PSE); alternative — secondary to combat TBI (PTE) |
| RCC / renal mass | Direct under *Combee* (TCE Group 1 + JP-4 OR ~3 + dioxin meta) **and** secondary to HTN |
| Incontinence | Secondary to stroke (post-stroke neurogenic bladder) |

A single internist or cardiologist letter can carry HTN/IHD/stroke/incontinence; the renal mass benefits from urology/oncology authorship; the seizure benefits from neurology.
