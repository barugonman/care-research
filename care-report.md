## Levels of Care to Consider (Massachusetts Terms)

Understanding the care types matters because pricing and what insurance covers varies dramatically:

1. **Skilled Nursing Facility (SNF) / "Nursing Home" (Level II)** — 24/7 RN coverage, two-person transfers routine, full medical care. **Best fit for limited-mobility residents who fall or need lifting.** Average MA cost without Medicaid is roughly **$14,000–$15,500/mo** for a private room, less for semi-private. **MassHealth covers 100% of cost** for those who qualify financially.
2. **Assisted Living Residence (ALR) with Memory Care** — apartment-style; staff for ADL help, medication management, locked dementia unit. **Typical MA range $5,000–$10,500/mo.** MassHealth does NOT pay rent, but the **GAFC** program can cover ~$1,200/mo of personal-care costs at certified residences.
3. **Rest Home (Level IV)** — supervised group setting; lower medical intensity than nursing home, 24-hour monitoring. **Generally the cheapest licensed 24-hour option** but limited bed availability and may not accept residents who need two-person transfers.
4. **Adult Foster Care (AFC) / Group Adult Foster Care (GAFC)** — caregiver in a private home or congregate setting; **MassHealth pays a tax-free stipend (~$1,000–$1,600/mo to caregiver)**; an option if a family member could host him.
5. **Continuing Care Retirement Community (CCRC)** — large entrance fee ($100K+) plus monthly. **Likely outside the budget** but listed for completeness.

## Financial Assistance — Pursue These in Parallel

Given the budget constraint, **financial-assistance enrollment should start immediately** because waiting periods, asset look-back, and bed availability all introduce delay.

### MassHealth (Medicaid) Long-Term Care
- **Asset limit (2026):** $2,000 countable for a single applicant. Home is generally non-countable while occupied; irrevocable funeral trusts and Medicaid-compliant annuities also non-countable.
- **Income:** No hard limit, but resident must contribute nearly all income to cost of care, keeping only **$72.80/mo personal needs allowance** plus Medicare premium.
- **Functional eligibility:** "Nursing Facility Level of Care" — needs help with ADLs (mobility, bathing, dressing, eating, toileting). **Your father's profile clearly qualifies.**
- **Look-back period:** 60 months. Asset transfers below fair market value within this window create penalty periods.
- **Estate recovery:** State can recover from the estate after death. Discuss with an elder-law attorney.
- **Apply via:** MassHealth Customer Service **800-841-2900**.

### Group Adult Foster Care (GAFC)
- For **assisted-living settings**. Reimburses the residence ~$1,200/mo for personal care. Resident still pays room/board.
- **Eligibility:** ~133% Federal Poverty Level income, $2,000 countable assets, needs help with 2+ ADLs.
- **Entitlement program** (no waitlist for benefit) — but you need a bed at a **GAFC-certified residence**. Ask each AL facility above whether they are GAFC-certified.

### Adult Foster Care (AFC)
- If a **family member** can house and care for him, MassHealth pays the family caregiver:
  - **Level I:** ~$1,000/mo (one ADL, supervision/cueing)
  - **Level II:** up to ~$1,600/mo (hands-on with 2+ ADLs)
- Tax-free stipend; includes nurse oversight and care manager support.
- Worth considering if a family member is in a position to provide care at home and the home can be made walker-accessible.

### Veterans Benefits
- If wartime veteran: **Veterans Home at Chelsea** (above) or **VA Aid & Attendance** pension benefit (currently up to ~$2,300/mo for a single veteran needing ADL help).
- Apply via VA or a county Veterans Service Officer (free help).

### North Shore Elder Services (Local ASAP)
- **Single most useful first phone call.** They are the state-funded Aging Services Access Point covering Marblehead, Salem, Peabody, Danvers, and Middleton.
- They provide **free** case-management, benefit screening, and placement guidance.
- Address: 152 Sylvan Street, Danvers, MA 01923 · Phone: **978-750-4540** (verify current number).

## Action Plan — Recommended Order of Operations

1. **Call North Shore Elder Services** for free intake; they can run the eligibility math and shortlist GAFC-certified beds.
2. **Check veteran status.** If eligible, request an application packet from Veterans Home at Chelsea — long waitlist potential, so apply early.
3. **Apply for MassHealth Long-Term Care** in parallel (60-month look-back means delay is costly). Consult an MA elder-law attorney before any asset transfers — fees are typically modest relative to potential savings/penalty exposure.
4. **Tour the top three nearby SNFs** that take MassHealth: **Lafayette Rehabilitation, Alliance Health at Devereux, and Phillips Manor**. Ask specifically about: semi-private room availability, two-person transfer protocols, dementia unit security, current MassHealth bed availability, waitlist length.
5. **Tour 1–2 mid-tier assisted-living/memory-care options** as backup: **Bertram House of Swampscott** (closest), **Charter Senior Living of Danvers** (all-inclusive memory care), **John Bertram House** (small, lowest entry price). Ask about GAFC certification.
6. **If a family member can host:** evaluate **Adult Foster Care** — likely the cheapest path that keeps him in family environment.

## Questions to Ask Every Facility on Tour

- Do you accept MassHealth for new residents, and how many MassHealth beds are currently available?
- What is the current waitlist for a semi-private/companion room?
- Are you GAFC-certified (assisted living only)?
- How do you handle a resident who falls and cannot get up — do you have mechanical lifts and staff trained in two-person transfers?
- Is your memory-care unit secured? Wander alarms? Locked elevator/exit?
- What is the **all-in monthly cost** for a semi-private room at his approximate care level — including base rate, care-level fees, medication management, and incontinence supplies?
- What happens if private funds run out — can he remain on MassHealth?
- What is the staff-to-resident ratio on the dementia unit during day, evening, and overnight shifts?
- Do you require a minimum private-pay period before accepting MassHealth? (Some communities require 1–3 years private pay first.)

## Important Caveats

- **Distances are estimates** based on standard map data; verify with Google Maps for routing and traffic.
- **Pricing is from third-party listing aggregators**, often a year or more old, and rarely reflects the all-in monthly cost a resident actually pays. **Always confirm current pricing in writing during a tour.**
- **MassHealth bed availability changes weekly** at every nursing home. A facility's willingness to accept MassHealth in principle does not guarantee a MassHealth-funded bed is open today.
- **Some assisted-living communities require 1–3 years of private-pay before they will accept GAFC reimbursement** — ask explicitly.
- **An elder-law attorney consultation is strongly recommended** before any asset planning given the 60-month MassHealth look-back period and estate recovery rules.
