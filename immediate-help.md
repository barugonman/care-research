**Tier definitions (uniform across MA agencies):**
- **Companion / homemaker** — non-medical; *will not* cover toileting. Insufficient for this case.
- **Home Health Aide (HHA)** — 75-hr state-trained; covers toileting, bathing, transfers, incontinence care. **This is what John needs.**
- **CNA** — 100-hr; HHA tasks + some monitored clinical.
- **RN** — meds, wound care, catheter; rarely needed overnight.

**Two MA-specific things to know before signing an agreement:**
1. **4-hr visit minimum** is standard at almost every agency.
2. The **MA Domestic Workers' Bill of Rights** (M.G.L. c. 149 § 190) requires that any caregiver on duty ≥24 consecutive hours can have up to 8 hours of sleep time excluded from pay *only with prior written agreement*. Without it, every meal/sleep hour is paid. Workers ≥40 hrs/wk must get 24 consecutive rest hours per week and 48 consecutive rest hours per calendar month. This is why "live-in" is harder to staff in MA than in lower-regulation states — agencies often default to the more expensive shift-based "awake" model.

**Recommendation for the family:** call BrightStar (VA-contracted), Right at Home (caring.com "Super Star"), and BAYADA (Medicare-certified) **in parallel on Day 1–2**. Whoever can start soonest with a competent overnight HHA wins. Don't sign an agency contract until you've asked: (a) earliest start, (b) caregiver consistency policy (will it be the same person each night?), (c) Hoyer/transfer training, (d) PD-care experience, (e) VA H/HHA contract status, (f) what happens if the assigned caregiver is sick.

## 5. Track C — Bridge & Hybrid Options

### a) Medicare post-acute home health — **the fastest free help if applicable**
If John has had a hospital admission for the stroke or seizure within the **last 30–60 days**, Medicare pays 100% of: skilled nursing, PT, OT, ST, plus **HHA aide hours (up to 28 hrs/wk combined with skilled visits)**. 60-day episode, renewable as long as a physician re-certifies need. **Zero out-of-pocket, no waitlist, starts in 48–72 hrs.**

Certified MA North Shore Medicare home-health agencies:
- **BAYADA Home Health Care** — Medicare-certified; [bayada.com](https://www.bayada.com)
- **VNA Care** — [vnacare.org](https://vnacare.org)
- **Care Dimensions** — [caredimensions.org](https://www.caredimensions.org)
- **Beverly Hospital / Beth Israel Lahey Health home-care unit** — 978-922-3000

Limitation: aide hours tied to skilled visits; capped at 28 hrs/wk; does NOT cover overnight. But it offsets a huge slice of daytime aide cost while in the window.

> **Action this week:** confirm with the family whether there was a stroke/seizure hospitalization in the last 60 days. If yes and Medicare home-health was declined at discharge, the family can still self-refer. Call BAYADA or VNA Care directly — RN comes within 48 hrs to do the OASIS assessment.

### b) Respite stays — fastest path to a bed
Respite beds are designed for 1–4 week turnover, so availability is consistently better than long-term placement. Useful as a **bridge** while setting up overnight aides at home (grab bars, hospital bed via Medicare DME, Hoyer if needed) and finishing the AgeSpan / VA paperwork.

Top respite candidates in this area: **Devereux House** (Marblehead, explicitly markets respite), **The Mariner Marblehead** (1–2 wk respite available), **Lafayette Rehabilitation** (Marblehead, short-term rehab beds), **Otterbein Marblehead Senior Life** (CCRC with internal step-up).

### c) Adult day health
Daytime supervision (6–10 hrs/day) including bathing assistance, meals, RN oversight, social/PT activities. Doesn't solve overnight but reduces daytime aide hours the family needs to fund.

- **Element Care PACE** (Lynn HQ; day centers in Lynn, Beverly, Gloucester, Lawrence): all Medicare + Medicaid integrated under one capitated payment; ~12–25 hrs/wk in-home aide hours **scale to need**. 877-803-5564 · [elementcare.org](https://elementcare.org)
- **North Shore Adult Day Health** (Beverly) — MassHealth-payable
- **Active Day of Peabody** — MassHealth-payable

### d) Adult Foster Care (AFC) — high-value if a child can move in
MassHealth pays a **tax-free** stipend to a non-spouse adult caregiver who provides 24-hr care in either the caregiver's home or the elder's home, with the caregiver living in. 2026 rates: Level I ~$1,000/mo, **Level II up to ~$1,600/mo** (toileting + transfer assistance qualifies). Stack with VA Aid & Attendance ($2,427/mo) and VDC ($1,800–$3,000/mo) — combined ~$5,000–$7,000/mo flows to the household, which funds outside overnight relief 2–3 nights/wk.

**MA AFC sponsor agencies (North Shore):** Northeast Arc (Danvers, 978-624-2300), Careforth (866-989-9474), Royale Care AFC, GSSSI.

**Caveat:** AFC requires the caregiver to live with the elder. If neither Dan nor Ali can move in (or have John move in with them), AFC doesn't fit. If either can, it is the single most leveraged subsidy.

### e) PACE — Element Care (worth a serious look if dual-eligible)
PACE bundles Medicare + Medicaid into one capitated payment with a primary-care team, transportation, adult day health, in-home aide, drugs, specialists. Marblehead is in Element Care's service area. Must meet nursing-facility level of care (John clearly will), be 55+, and be safely served in the community. The PACE day center reduces daytime aide need substantially, freeing private-pay budget for overnight coverage.

## 6. The Funding Stack — How to Pay for Either Track

Two illustrative paths, both built around stacking VA + MassHealth + family:

### Path 1 — Stay home with 12-hr overnight aide + family days
- 12-hr overnight aide × 7 nights × $38/hr ≈ **−$11,900/mo gross**
- Less VA Aid & Attendance: **+$2,427/mo**
- Less VA Homemaker/HHA (covers ~16 hrs/wk daytime; budget value ~$2,400/mo): **+$2,400**
- Less Frail Elder Waiver (~20 hrs/wk personal care; budget value ~$3,000/mo): **+$3,000**
- Less VDC (if adult child paid as caregiver, $2,000–$3,000/mo stipend back to family): **+$2,000–$3,000**
- **Net family out-of-pocket: ~$1,000–$3,000/mo**

### Path 2 — AFC w/ adult child moved in + part-time outside overnight relief
- AFC stipend to child: **+$1,600/mo tax-free**
- VA A&A: **+$2,427/mo**
- VDC: **+$2,000–$3,000/mo** (check with VA social worker whether combinable with AFC)
- Outside overnight relief 2 nights/wk × 12 hr × $38/hr ≈ **−$5,700/mo**
- **Net: roughly break-even to modestly positive** for the family — the caregiving child is also collecting income.

### Path 3 — MassHealth SNF semi-private (sustainable long term)
Once John spends down to the $2,000 asset limit (consult elder-law counsel — see [legal-review.md](legal-review.md)), MassHealth covers a semi-private SNF bed 100%. **Net family out-of-pocket: $0 monthly**, though John contributes nearly all his income (Social Security, pension, VA pension) to the cost of care, retaining only the **$72.80/mo personal needs allowance** plus Medicare premium. This is the cheapest sustainable solution if home care becomes unaffordable. Note: VA Aid & Attendance is **reduced to $90/mo** when the veteran is in a MassHealth-paid SNF.

## 7. Decision Framework — Home vs. Facility

The math alone doesn't decide it; quality-of-life factors do. Use this checklist:

**Choose stay-at-home with overnight aide if:**
- One adult child is willing and able to take daytime responsibility most days
- John is generally lucid (which appears true) and the home environment matters to him
- The house can be made safe (grab bars, hospital bed, possible Hoyer; flood/smoke detectors current)
- Family can absorb $1,000–$3,000/mo out-of-pocket on top of subsidies
- The hourly aide demand stays below ~50 hrs/wk total

**Choose AFC with adult child moved in if:**
- One child can plausibly move in (or have John move in with them) for an extended period
- The caregiver child is comfortable with hands-on toileting/transfer work (or wants to learn — sponsor agency provides RN-led training)
- The family stack ($1,600 AFC + $2,427 A&A + $2,000–$3,000 VDC) is meaningful to them

**Choose assisted living / memory care if:**
- Home-care hourly demand has climbed past ~50 hrs/wk
- John needs more supervision than overnight alone solves (e.g., wandering risk, medication missteps)
- The family can fund $5–$10K/mo gap between facility cost and SS/pension income
- A GAFC-certified AL is available (cuts ~$1,200/mo)

**Choose SNF semi-private (MassHealth track) if:**
- Two-person transfer becomes necessary regularly
- John is recurrently falling
- 24-hr awake nursing is needed (not just overnight aide presence)
- Spend-down to MassHealth eligibility is on the horizon anyway

**Veterans Home at Chelsea is the highest-quality / lowest-cost residential option** if it opens up. File now regardless — the wait can be months but the income-based pricing is dramatically below private SNF.
